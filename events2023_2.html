Quiz competition on international day of women and girls in science
The International Day of Women and Girls in Science is celebrated annually on February 11th to recognize the critical role that women and girls play in science and technology. The day aims to promote gender equality and the empowerment of women and girls in the fields of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).
The day was established by the United Nations General Assembly in 2015, and since then, it has become an important platform for highlighting the achievements of women and girls in STEM fields and promoting opportunities for their participation and advancement.
The theme of the International Day of Women and Girls in Science varies each year, but it always focuses on the need to encourage and support women and girls to pursue careers in STEM fields. Through various initiatives, events, and activities, the day aims to raise awareness of the challenges faced by women and girls in these fields and to inspire a new generation of female scientists, engineers, and innovators.
Overall, the International Day of Women and Girls in Science serves as a powerful reminder of the vital contributions that women and girls make to scientific progress and innovation, and the importance of promoting gender equality in all areas of society.
